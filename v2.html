Track 01The Muster

Join the rally right

Which slot, which heroes, and what fills the march. Get these three right before anything else.

123

Joiner goes in slot 1

Your joiner hero takes the first slot - the one carrying the little flag icon. That is the hero the rally reads.

Slots 2 and 3 are for XP

The other two heroes do not change the rally bonus, but give them a high XP level so you bring more troops.

More archers = more damage

Make sure every single march you send into a Bear Hunt has archers in it. The why is in Formations.

Track 02One Shot

The only skill that matters

As a joiner, exactly one number on your hero counts. Everything else is noise.

It is always the Expedition skill on the top-right of your joiner hero - that skill and its skill level are all the rally reads.

Gear on joiners does nothing

Strip the gear off your joiner heroes and feed it into your three rally leaders instead. See the gear guide.

Joiner cap 4 max

Only up to four joiners contribute to the overall rally bonus.

Track 03The Pecking Order

Skills skip, stars don't count

Higher skill levels skip lower ones, and troop capacity comes from XP - never from stars.

5Skips Lv 1, 2, 3 & 4Active
4Skipped by Lv 5Skipped
3Skipped by Lv 4+Skipped
1Skipped by anything higherSkipped

Stars are irrelevant

It does not matter how many stars a hero has. If the skill level is lower than the other joiners, it gets skipped.

XP = troop capacity

Always join with your highest XP-level hero. More XP means more troops, and more troops means more points.

Example: Jaeger

Usually don't join with Jaeger. But you can purely for capacity - his Lv3 skill is skipped by any Lv4+ joiner anyway.

So you can ride along

A low-skill hero is fine in a strong rally only when its skill will be skipped - you are there for the troops, not the buff.

Track 05The Board

The tier list, and who to avoid

These heroes either roll the dice or do nothing at all. Keep them out of real rallies.

Rosa, Marlin, Petra, Jaeger, JabelDo not call

Chance-based. Their damage does not stack and the output is randomly generated.

Zoe, Saul, Alcar, EricNo damage

They only have a defensive skill - they don't increase damage at all.

Any blue (R) heroesGathering only

Their skills only help gather resources. Nothing for a rally.

DianaNiche

Only useful against rallying terrors - not Bear Hunt.

Quinn, Howard, Gordon, FahdDefensive only

Defensive skill only - no rally damage.

Exception: ThrudArcher rallies

Thrud sits in the avoid tier, but she's great for archer-heavy rallies - she boosts all archer damage. Everyone else in the avoid tier: genuinely avoid.

Track 06Ballistics

Different heroes hit harder

Same joiners add up. Different joiners multiply. Mixing stat families is free damage.

# each unique effect_op multiplies separately
damageUp = math.prod(1 + v/100 for v in DamageUp.values())

same op -> {102: 100} -> 1 + 100/100 = 2.0
two ops -> {101: 50, 102: 50} -> 1.5 * 1.5 = 2.25

That's a 12.5% relative increase just from mixing instead of stacking one hero. Same op, the buffs add. Different ops, they compound.

Track 07The Scales

Balance the rally

Two jobs: joiners read what a rally needs, leaders police what's in theirs.

Attack

Margot & Amane. Aim for 2 of 4.

Lethality

Chenko & Yeonwoo. Aim for 2 of 4.

If you're a joiner

Read what the rally needs. If it already has a Chenko & Yeonwoo, join with Margot or Amane (and vice versa). Save your Hilde or low-skill hero for weaker rallies. Stop dumping trash joiners or tiny troop counts into strong rallies.

If you're a rally leader

Monitor your own rally. Kick any hero from the avoid list. Speak up in chat if it's unbalanced. Watch your active rally bonuses - aim for 2/4 attack and 2/4 lethality.

But don't over-kick

Do not kick someone for the "wrong" joiner hero if their joiner skill isn't active - it was skipped (see Track 03).

Track 08The Field Plan
